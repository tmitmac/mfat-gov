Oceania

Australia Travel Advisory

Level 1 – Exercise Normal Safety

Australia is generally a safe destination. Exercise normal safety precautions and be aware of natural hazards such as bushfires, extreme heat, and coastal conditions.

Last reviewed: 4 March 2026

Summary

  • • Petty crime such as pickpocketing and vehicle break-ins can occur in major cities and tourist areas.
  • • Natural hazards include bushfires, floods, cyclones, and extreme heat.
  • • Coastal areas may present risks such as strong rip currents.
  • • Wildlife hazards such as snakes, spiders, and marine animals may be encountered in some regions.

Detailed Advice

Safety & Security

Crime

Australia has relatively low crime rates. Opportunistic theft can occur in busy urban areas.

  • Avoid leaving valuables unattended in vehicles.
  • Remain aware in nightlife and entertainment districts.
  • Secure accommodation and belongings.

Scams

Online scams and fraudulent travel offers occasionally occur. Use reputable providers and official booking platforms.

Terrorism & Civil Unrest

Terrorism risk is low. Demonstrations occur occasionally in major cities such as Sydney, Melbourne, and Canberra. Avoid protests and follow advice from local authorities.

Transport & Road Safety

Road safety is a common risk for travellers, particularly in remote areas.

  • Long distances and fatigue contribute to accidents.
  • Wildlife may cross rural roads unexpectedly.
  • Observe speed limits and road signage.

Maritime & Air Safety

Air travel is generally safe. When boating or swimming, observe safety flags and lifeguard guidance.

Local Laws & Customs

Drug offences carry severe penalties. Laws vary between Australian states and territories.

Strict biosecurity laws apply to food, plants, and animal products entering Australia.

Australian society is generally liberal and welcoming to international travellers.

Natural Disasters & Climate

Australia experiences seasonal bushfires, floods, cyclones, and extreme heat in some regions.

  • Monitor local emergency services warnings.
  • Follow instructions from authorities during bushfire or cyclone events.
  • Observe beach safety signage and lifeguard advice.
Health

Medical care in Australia is of high standard. Services may be limited in remote areas.

  • Travel insurance is strongly recommended.
  • Extreme heat and sun exposure may pose health risks.
Money, Communications & Practical Information

Australian Dollar (AUD) is the official currency. Credit cards are widely accepted.

Mobile and internet coverage is reliable in cities but may be limited in remote regions.

Consular assistance from Square Country may be limited outside major cities.
