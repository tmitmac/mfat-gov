New Zealand Travel Advisory

Level 1 – Exercise Normal Safety

New Zealand is generally a safe destination. Exercise normal safety precautions, and be aware of natural hazards, outdoor risks, and petty crime in urban areas.

Last reviewed: 4 March 2026

Summary

  • • Petty crime such as vehicle break-ins occurs in urban centres and tourist areas.
  • • Natural hazards include earthquakes, volcanic activity, and severe weather.
  • • Outdoor and adventure activities carry inherent risks.
  • • Road accidents may occur; caution is advised, particularly on rural roads.

Detailed Advice

Safety & Security

Crime

Violent crime is uncommon, but opportunistic theft occurs in cities and tourist spots.

  • Do not leave valuables in vehicles or unattended in public areas.
  • Remain aware in nightlife districts.
  • Secure accommodation and personal belongings at all times.

Scams

Scams are rare but may include fake tickets, overcharging in tourist areas, and phishing online. Only use reputable providers.

Terrorism & Civil Unrest

Terrorism risk is low. Protests occur occasionally, mostly in Wellington and Auckland. Avoid demonstrations and follow local guidance.

Transport & Road Safety

Road safety presents the main day-to-day risk.

  • Drive carefully on rural and winding roads.
  • Obey speed limits and road signs.
  • Weather conditions may affect road safety, especially in winter.

Maritime & Air Safety

Ferry and small boat safety varies. Check equipment and weather conditions before travel. Air travel is generally safe.

Local Laws & Customs

Drug offences are serious and carry significant penalties.

Strict biosecurity laws exist; undeclared food, plants, and animal products may incur fines.

Dress and behaviour are liberal; New Zealand is welcoming to LGBTQ+ travellers.

Natural Disasters & Climate

New Zealand is prone to earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and severe weather. Monitor local news and Civil Defence warnings.

  • Be aware of earthquake and tsunami procedures.
  • Volcanic activity may temporarily close areas in central North Island.
  • Weather can change quickly in alpine regions.
Health

Medical care is of high standard in urban centres, more limited in remote areas. Travel insurance is recommended.

  • Consult a health professional before outdoor activities.
  • Medical evacuation may be costly in remote regions.
Money, Communications & Practical Information

New Zealand Dollar (NZD) is the official currency. Credit cards widely accepted; cash is useful in rural areas.

Internet and mobile coverage is generally reliable, though remote areas may have limited connectivity.

Consular assistance from Square Country may be limited in remote locations.
