Vietnam Travel Advisory

Level 1 – Exercise Normal Safety

Exercise increased caution in Vietnam due to petty crime, road safety risks, severe seasonal weather, and the possibility of localised protests.

Last reviewed: 4 March 2026

Summary

  • • Petty crime including bag snatching and pickpocketing occurs in major cities.
  • • Traffic accidents are common due to high volumes of motorcycles.
  • • Demonstrations can occur with little warning.
  • • Typhoons and flooding may disrupt travel during wet season.

Detailed Advice

Safety & Security

Crime

Petty crime is common in major urban centres. Bag snatching by motorcyclists and phone theft occur frequently in Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City. Foreign visitors are often targeted in tourist districts.

  • Do not carry bags loosely on the street side.
  • Avoid displaying expensive jewellery or electronics.
  • Remain alert in crowded markets and transport hubs.

Scams

Common scams include overcharging by taxi drivers, fake tour operators, counterfeit goods, and online romance scams. Only use reputable service providers and confirm prices in advance.

Violent Crime

Violent crime against foreign nationals is rare but has occurred. Alcohol consumption increases vulnerability. Avoid isolated areas after dark.

Terrorism

There is no recent history of terrorist attacks in Vietnam. However, global terrorism remains a risk and attacks could occur without warning.

Civil Unrest

Protests are uncommon but may occur over political or labour issues. Authorities respond quickly. Avoid demonstrations and follow local instructions.

Transport & Road Safety

Road safety presents the greatest day-to-day risk to travellers.

  • Traffic accidents are common, especially involving motorcycles.
  • Road rules may not be consistently observed.
  • Pedestrian crossings are not always respected.

Only use reputable transport providers. If riding a motorcycle, wear a helmet at all times. Ensure you hold an appropriate licence and insurance coverage.

Maritime Safety

Ferry and boat safety standards vary. Confirm safety equipment is available before departure. Weather conditions can deteriorate quickly.

Local Laws & Customs

Drug Offences

Penalties for drug offences are extremely severe and may include life imprisonment or the death penalty, even for small quantities.

Arrest & Detention

If arrested, you may not have immediate access to legal representation or consular assistance. Prison conditions can be basic.

Dual Nationality

If you are a dual national of Vietnam, local authorities may treat you as a Vietnamese citizen and limit consular access.

Cultural Expectations

Dress modestly at religious sites. Remove shoes before entering temples and private homes.

LGBTQ+ Travellers

Same-sex relationships are legal. However, public attitudes may be conservative outside major cities.

Natural Disasters & Climate

Vietnam is prone to typhoons, flooding, and landslides, particularly from May to November.

  • Monitor weather updates during typhoon season.
  • Follow local authority instructions.
  • Expect transport disruptions.

Extreme heat and high humidity may pose health risks. Air quality in major cities can be poor at times.

Health

Medical facilities in major cities are improving but may not meet international standards. Outside urban centres, services are limited.

  • Comprehensive travel insurance is essential.
  • Medical evacuation can be expensive.
  • Consult a health professional before travel.

Mosquito-borne illnesses such as malaria and dengue fever occur year-round. Take precautions to avoid insect bites.

Money, Communications & Practical Information

Currency

The local currency is the Vietnamese Dong (VND). Credit cards are widely accepted in major cities but cash is preferred in rural areas.

Internet & Communications

Internet access is widely available. Some online content may be restricted.

Consular Assistance

Square Country cannot provide legal advice, pay legal fees, or secure your release from detention. Our assistance may be limited in remote areas.
