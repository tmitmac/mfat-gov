Europe

United Kingdom Travel Advisory

Level 2 – Exercise Increased Caution

Exercise increased caution in the United Kingdom due to the ongoing threat of terrorism. Petty crime such as pickpocketing may occur in busy tourist areas.

Last reviewed: 4 March 2026

Summary

  • • Terrorist attacks remain possible.
  • • Petty crime such as pickpocketing and phone theft occurs in tourist areas.
  • • Demonstrations and protests may occur in major cities.
  • • Public transport networks may be disrupted during strikes or security incidents.

Detailed Advice

Safety & Security

Crime

Petty crime occurs in busy areas, especially in London and other large cities.

  • Watch for pickpocketing in crowded transport hubs.
  • Keep phones and valuables secure.
  • Avoid leaving belongings unattended in public places.

Scams

Scams targeting tourists occasionally occur, including fake ticket sales, fraudulent accommodation listings, and distraction theft.

Terrorism

There is an ongoing threat of terrorism in the United Kingdom. Attacks could occur with little warning.

  • Possible targets include transport hubs, tourist attractions, shopping centres, and public events.
  • Previous attacks have occurred in major cities including London and Manchester.
  • Remain alert in crowded places and follow the instructions of local authorities.

The UK government maintains a national terrorism threat level system. Security presence may be increased in public areas.

Transport & Road Safety

Public transport is generally reliable but can be crowded during peak hours.

  • Mind the gap on underground rail systems.
  • Transport strikes occasionally disrupt travel.
  • Traffic drives on the left side of the road.
Local Laws & Customs

Drug possession and trafficking carry serious penalties.

The United Kingdom is socially liberal and welcoming to international travellers.

Natural Disasters & Climate

Natural disasters are uncommon, though heavy rain and storms may occasionally cause flooding.

Health

Healthcare is of a high standard. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance.

Money, Communications & Practical Information

Pound Sterling (GBP) is the official currency. Credit cards are widely accepted.

Mobile and internet coverage is generally reliable.

Advisory Level
